# Data Streams

**Capture context in your container.**

Your life with AI produces a constant stream of context — conversations, notes,
voice memos, messages, files. Data Streams are the ways all of that flows *into* your
vault, where it's encrypted, organized, and made searchable. Everything funnels
through one private container that only you can open.

## One door in, by design

No matter where a piece of context comes from — an import, a chat message, a file
upload, a connected channel — it enters your vault through a **single point**. That
one choke-point is what guarantees the rule that makes Mycelium trustworthy:

> Anything that comes in is **saved, encrypted, and queued for understanding** — once,
> with no duplicates, and with nothing slipping past unencrypted.

You never have to wonder whether something was protected. There's only one path, and
it always protects.

## Ways context flows in

### Import your history

The biggest single boost is bringing your existing AI history home. Export your
conversations from **Claude** or **ChatGPT** and drag the `.zip` onto the Import
screen. Mycelium recognizes the format, brings in every message, and skips anything
it's already seen — so re-importing is always safe.

### Notes & documents

Write directly into your library, or drop in files: text, Markdown, PDFs, Word
documents, images. They become first-class memories you (and your AI) can search and
revise. Documents live at stable paths, so saving to the same place updates the
document rather than cluttering your vault with copies.

### Files & voice

Attach files and voice notes. Audio is transcribed and images are described **on your
own machine** — no cloud round-trip — so even your media stays private while becoming
searchable.

### Channels

Connect a messaging channel — **Telegram, Discord, WhatsApp** — and messages flow in
as you send and receive them. Photos, documents, and voice notes that arrive are
understood locally too, so your channel history becomes part of your memory without
anything leaving your box.

### Health

Sync **Apple Health** to bring in your body's data — sleep, heart-rate variability,
resting heart rate, steps, workouts. Your AI can then connect how you've been
*feeling* with what you've been *thinking*.

## What happens after capture

The instant something is captured, two quiet things happen in the background:

1. **It's embedded.** Mycelium computes a private, local "fingerprint" of the meaning
   of your text so it can be found by *what it means*, not just the words it contains.
2. **It's understood.** Mycelium tags entities and themes — the people, projects, and
   ideas inside it — so your memory has structure.

This all runs locally and best-effort. If the understanding step is busy, your data is
still safely stored and fully searchable in the meantime — nothing is ever lost
waiting in line.

## You're always in control

- **Mark something sensitive** and it's kept out of proactive recall and never
  published — it's there when you search for it, but your AI won't surface it on its
  own.
- **Pin something** and it floats to the top of what your AI sees first.
- **Forget something** and it's genuinely gone — the content is destroyed, its
  fingerprint wiped from search, and it's removed from the map. There's no undo,
  because "forget" should mean forget.
