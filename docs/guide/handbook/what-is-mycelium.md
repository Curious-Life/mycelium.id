# What is Mycelium?

**Mycelium is personal intelligence that belongs to you.**

You talk to AI more and more. It's starting to know you — your projects, your people,
the way you think. But that knowledge lives on someone else's servers, locked inside
one company's product, readable by them and gone the day you switch tools.

Mycelium flips that around. It's a private vault for your mind that runs **on your own
machine**. You feed it your conversations, notes, and files. It encrypts everything
with keys only you hold, organizes it, and then lets *any* AI you choose draw on it —
without ever handing that AI (or anyone) a copy of your data.

> Think of it as the soil under the forest. Mushrooms come and go on the surface —
> Claude today, something new tomorrow. The mycelium underneath is what remembers, and
> it's yours.

## Why it exists

Today, your memory is held hostage by whoever makes your AI:

- **It's trapped.** Everything Claude knows about you stays in Claude. Switch to a
  different model and you start over from zero.
- **It's readable.** Your most intimate data — what you think, who you love, what
  you're working through — sits in plaintext on a company's servers.
- **It's not yours.** You can't take it, back it up, or truly delete it. You're a
  tenant in someone else's building.

Mycelium gives you the opposite of all three: memory that's **portable, private, and
owned**.

## The four pillars

### 🔒 The Vault — your data is gold

Everything lives in **one encrypted database on your device**. Not the cloud — your
disk. The encryption keys are generated on your machine and never leave it. Even the
search index and the AI's working notes are encrypted. If someone copied your hard
drive, they'd get nothing but noise.

→ [Read about The Vault](the-vault.md)

### 🧠 Any Intelligence — bring your own brain

Mycelium isn't an AI. It's the **membrane** between you and whatever AI you want to
use. Connect Claude, ChatGPT, a local model, or all of them — they each get the same
rich memory of you through one open standard (the Model Context Protocol). Switch
models whenever you like. Your memory stays put and keeps compounding.

→ [Connect your AI](connect-your-ai.md)

### 🌊 Data Streams — capture context in your container

Your context flows in from everywhere: import your full Claude or ChatGPT history,
drop in documents and voice notes, connect a Telegram or Discord channel, sync your
Apple Health data. It all funnels into one private container, encrypted and searchable.

→ [Read about Data Streams](data-streams.md)

### 🍄 The Resonance Engine — grow connections that amplify

This is the part nobody else has. Mycelium doesn't just *store* your data — it
*measures* it. It maps your thoughts into territories and realms, watches which ideas
fire together, and surfaces patterns no single conversation could ever show you. It's
the difference between a filing cabinet and a map of your own mind.

→ [Read about the Resonance Engine](resonance-engine.md)

## What Mycelium is *not*

- **It is not a chatbot.** It doesn't think or talk on its own. It's a memory that
  answers when an AI asks. You always bring the intelligence.
- **It is not a cloud service.** There's no Mycelium server holding your data. There's
  no account to sign up for. It's an app on your computer.
- **It is not watching you.** No autonomous agent runs in the background making
  decisions. It does exactly what it's asked, and nothing more.

## Who it's for

People who think out loud with AI and don't want that thinking to vanish, leak, or be
locked to one vendor. Writers, builders, researchers, founders — anyone for whom their
own accumulated context is genuinely valuable, and worth owning.

## Is it free?

Yes. Mycelium is **free and open source** under the AGPL-3.0 license. Download it, run
it, read every line, fork it. The code is the product, and the code is yours. If it's
useful to you, you can [support its development](https://github.com/Curious-Life/mycelium.id) —
but you never have to.
