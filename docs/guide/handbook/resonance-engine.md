# The Resonance Engine

**Grow connections that amplify. See what your data holds.**

Most memory tools stop at storage and search: they keep your stuff and help you find
it again. Mycelium goes further. The Resonance Engine **measures** your thinking and
draws a living map of it — patterns no single conversation could ever reveal.

This is the part of Mycelium that's genuinely new.

## From a pile to a map

Imagine all your thoughts as points scattered in space. On their own they're just a
pile. The Resonance Engine finds the structure hiding in that pile:

```
Territories   Clusters of related thinking — your recurring subjects.
              "The festival project." "Feelings about my dad."
              "That recurring question about meaning."

Realms        The big neighborhoods those territories group into.
              The handful of major themes your mind keeps returning to.

Co-firing     Which territories light up together — even when you'd never
              consciously connect them. The hidden bridges in your mind.
```

The map isn't fixed. As you think and capture more, territories **split, merge, grow,
and fade**. It's a portrait of your mind that updates as your mind moves.

## The signature of how you think

Beyond *what* you think about, the Resonance Engine measures *how* your thinking moves
over time — its rhythm and momentum. From these dynamics it can tell when you're:

- **Stable** — settled in a groove.
- **Cycling** — circling the same loop.
- **Exploring** — ranging widely across new ground.
- **Transforming** — in the middle of a real shift.

It can notice a change in your cognitive weather *before it's obvious to you* — a
gentle "something's shifting" rather than a dashboard of vanity metrics.

## What you can do with it

- **Ask your AI about your patterns.** *"What themes have I been circling lately?"*
  *"What connects to the thing I'm stuck on?"* The answers come from the actual shape
  of your thinking, not a guess.
- **Explore the map directly.** The app's Mindscape view renders your realms and
  territories as a 3D landscape you can wander through.
- **Find the bridges.** Surface the unexpected links — the territory that keeps firing
  alongside a totally different one — and follow the thread.

## Built in the open, on purpose

The science underneath the Resonance Engine — the clustering, the co-firing analysis,
the rhythm metrics — is **open source**. We chose that deliberately. Storage is a
commodity and everyone's building memory; the measurement layer is the real frontier,
and a frontier is more valuable shared than hoarded. The algorithms are free to copy;
what they can't copy is *your* months and years of accumulated history, and what your
particular map means.

## When does it appear?

The map needs material to work with. On a brand-new vault, the topology views will
tell you they're not ready yet and point you to import and build. Once you've brought
in enough history and Mycelium has computed your mindscape, the realms, territories,
and rhythms come to life — and keep refining themselves from there.
