# Shared Spaces

**Connect through resonance.**

> **Status: roadmap.** The foundations are being built; the social layer described
> here is the direction, not a shipped feature yet. Mycelium V1 ships *sovereignty
> first* — a vault that's fully yours. Shared Spaces is what grows on top of it.

Everything in Mycelium so far has been about **you and your vault**. Shared Spaces is
about what becomes possible when many private vaults can connect — without any of them
giving up privacy.

## The vision

No ads. No feed engineered to keep you scrolling. No algorithm deciding who you should
see. Instead:

- **Find people you actually resonate with.** Because Mycelium understands the *shape*
  of your thinking, it can find others whose maps genuinely overlap with yours — not
  by reading your data, but by comparing patterns privately.
- **Scope your boundaries.** You decide exactly what's shareable and what stays
  sealed. Connection is opt-in, granular, and revocable.
- **Cultivate shared spaces.** Grow a common ground with the people and communities
  you choose — a place where context is shared deliberately, not harvested.

## Privacy doesn't break when you connect

The whole point of Shared Spaces is that connecting **doesn't** mean exposing your
vault. Matching happens through privacy-preserving comparison of patterns, not by
anyone reading anyone else's data. Sharing is always something you *do on purpose*, a
piece at a time — never a default, never a leak.

Your vault stays sovereign. You simply gain the option to let parts of it touch other
sovereign vaults, on your terms.

## How it connects

Mycelium connects vaults the way the open web connects sites — through an **open
federation protocol**, not a central server. Each vault has its own identity and can
request, accept, or decline connections to others. There's no Mycelium-the-company
sitting in the middle; nodes talk to nodes.

That openness is the strategy: every new vault makes the network more valuable, and
because the protocol is free to implement, the network can only grow. One vault is
useful on its own. Many vaults, resonating, become something new.

## Today

You can already publish individual documents from your vault to your own public page —
a deliberate, one-at-a-time act of sharing, fully under your control. The richer
social layer (discovery, private matching, shared spaces) is in active design.
